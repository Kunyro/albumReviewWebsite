Endtroducing

DJ Shadow

1996

Genre: Instrumental Hip-hop

Label: Universal UMC

Reviewed: December 1, 2022

9

Reviewed by lilpiri

Peak Instrumental Hip-hop

Masterpiece of assembled sound. When I listen to this album I think about how the drums are so central to it, and most of the songs are defined by how the drums sound and what they're doing, that "student of the drums..." clip being one of the first things you hear is very intentional. I've been coming back to it for a good decade now and I've gotten new things from it as time goes on so it's really a lasting personal favorite. I love how Midnight In A Perfect World has a vocal sample that isn't revealed to be super corny until the very end. The stretch of Changeling - Transmission 1 - What Does Your Soul Look Like Pt 4 is certainly one of my favorite 13 minutes in music. When the saxophone comes in on Soul Pt 4... I float off to a different planet when that saxophone comes in. That is probably my favorite beat ever and a truly sublime piece of music. If there's an album for max volume in the car driving through downtown at 4am it's this and Burial. Everybody knows that! The fast drum part in Stem/Long Stem has never really done anything for me though but beyond that it's god level heady music. It's pretty incredible that this album has the status it does because this guy with like two singles out puts together a bunch of samples off records he found in the basement of a single record store in Sacramento and here it's rated higher than Blood on the Tracks and Rubber Soul and In A Silent Way. But I get it, there's a remarkable depth that is otherworldly and very addictive. When I think about what his music sounds like now and how he never made anything close to this level again, it makes me think there was some sort of divine energy that was channeled through him to make these specific samples be found and put in this arrangement with each other, like it was meant to happen and he was merely the vessel for it. A marvelous and singular piece of music that exists in its own world.

e: what else sounds like this? This is an eternal album. It will always sound like it's from the future. The fact that the source records were all from the same record store's basement and fit together like puzzle pieces makes me think this was meant to happen, I will be playing this on the nice headphones at 3am when I'm 90
